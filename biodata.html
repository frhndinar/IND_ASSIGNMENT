It's My Biodata!!

I was born at Hospital Sungai Petani, Kedah. Now I lived at Gurun Kedah. Because I am staying at Kedah, I want to share some of my favorite places in Kedah with my readers. Kedah is in northern Malaysia, not far from the Thailand border. It is known as Malaysia's "rice bowl" since it is the birthplace of half of the country's rice. In addition to being an important agricultural region, it is also the location of one of Malaysia's most visited islands: Langkawi.


The first one is Zahir Mosque. As the oldest and largest mosque in Malaysia, Zahir Mosque was constructed in 1912. This mosque was also included in a list of the world's most beautiful mosques. The design has five primary sections, each of which stands for one of the five pillars of Islam. The dome may seem black from a distance, but a closer inspection reveals that it is really a deep ruby-red color.


Second, clock tower. This clock tower was erected in 1912 to alert locals that it was time for prayer. The clock tower is one of the oldest tourist destinations in Kedah, and it continues to serve its original purpose of keeping time even in the modern period. The building's fusion of Islamic and colonial styles is remarkable, too.


Lastly, the paddy museum. It seems sensible that the people of Malaysia would create a museum to honor their national staple crop. The museum's three stories encompass 12,000 square feet and include various exhibits and information. The museum's roof has a paddy plant pattern that stands out from the crowd.
